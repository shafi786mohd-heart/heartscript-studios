1 Feature-Spewing SKU Cataloging vs. Solution-Based Context

Current Catalog Flaw

Presenting items purely as SKU numbers (e.g., "Dry AC Model V2") forces busy architects to calculate compatibility themselves. Standard elevators have zero-drain rules; they buy compliance, not BTUs.

Our Modern Redesign Fix

Organizing the layout around specific architectural bottlenecks (e.g. "Safe overhead water-free cooling"). We map compliance criteria directly inside tabbed technology groups.

2 High-Friction Static Downloads vs. Interactive Configurator Flows

Current Catalog Flaw

Relying on static PDF downloads and blank "Contact Us" forms. Elevator consultants are busy and expect instantaneous technical confirmation of shaft constraints.

Our Modern Redesign Fix

An interactive self-service spec configurator. Users specify elevator counts and mechanical constraints to obtain instant sizing results and calculated B2B value outputs.

3 Zero Search / Answer Engine Optimization (AEO/GEO) Readiness

Current Catalog Flaw

Online lists contain sparse, thin keywords. They fail to rank for complex systems queries and are invisible to conversational systems (ChatGPT, Gemini) that aggregate providers based on authority schemas.

Our Modern Redesign Fix

Structured Schema.org entity metadata injected directly in-header, supported by a semantic Engineering Library (detailed HEPA air re-evaporation and CCTV physics articles) establishing technical authority.
